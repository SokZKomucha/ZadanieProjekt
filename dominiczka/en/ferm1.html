What is alcohol fermentation?

Alcohol fermentation is a biological process in which yeast or other microorganisms convert sugars, such as glucose or sucrose, into ethyl alcohol and carbon dioxide. It is one of the most well-known forms of fermentation, used in the production of alcoholic beverages and in various industries.

How does it work?

Alcohol fermentation is a process where enzymes in yeast break down sugars into simpler molecules. These molecules are then converted into ethyl alcohol and carbon dioxide. In the process, energy is released, allowing the yeast to survive in such harsh conditions.

Applications of alcohol fermentation

Alcohol fermentation has a wide range of applications in the production of alcoholic beverages. It is used in the process of making wine, beer, cider, and other alcoholic drinks. Additionally, the ethyl alcohol produced through alcohol fermentation is utilized in the chemical industry for the production of solvents, perfumes, cosmetics, and alternative fuels, such as biofuels.
